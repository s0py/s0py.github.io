Digestive System

Eatin' 'n' Poopin'

Digestion begins with the mouth
(not pictured.) Food will procede from the mouth to the esophagus where it is broguht to the stomach by peristalsis. The stomach begins the breakdown of food after which it enter the small intestine.
Nutrients from the food get abosrbed into the body and the food then carries on to the large intestine ,this is where water and other unabsorbed nutrients get absorbed. The liquid waste travels to the bladder and is then expelled from the body at the cloaca along with the solid waste.
The liver produces bile and is the largest organ. Said bile is secreted by the gall bladder. Bile from the liver and insulin from the pancreas (not labeled) aid in digestion. The spleen makes and stores as well as removes blood cells.

Nervous System

Thinking and Stuff

The Nervous System consists of 3 parts, the Central Nervous System, the Peripheral Nervous System, and the Autonomic Nervous System. The Central Nervous System consists of the brain and spinal chord. The brain consists of the Fore, Mid, and Hind regions. The Fore containing cerebral hemispheres and olfactory lobes which control smell, the Mid containing optic lobes which are invloved in sight, and the Hind containing the medula oblongata which controls automatic functions such as digestion. The Peripheral nervous system contains 10 pairs of cranial nerves (not pictured) and 12 spinal nerves. The spinal nerves pass information from the brain to the extremities of the body. the Autonomic Nervous System consists of the ganglia (not labled).

Circulatory&Respiratory

Bloods and Things

A frog's skin (not pictured) is permeable to H2O, CO2, and O2. When a frog is submerged it uses it's skin, which is protected by a slimey substance and contains blood vessels, to take in O. Whenthe frog is on land, it uses buccal pumping. A process in which the frog draws in air from its nostrils to the buccal cavity which then compresses and pushes the air into the lungs.
Frogs have 3 chambered hearts. The blood is drawn in from atria where it then enters the ventricle and is pumped into the aorta if oxygenated and the pulmonary artery for deoxygenated blood which enters the lungs. Blood is brought to the kidneys where it is cleaned. Multiple veins and arteries such as the gastric artery and the jugular vein carry blood throughout the frog. (Other examples include the subcalvian artery for the arm and shoulder) The conus arteriosus is a major artery that blood exits from.

Reproductive System

Like the number after 5?

The urinary and reproductive systems of a frog are interconnected. In a male frog the sperm are produce in the testis and flow in semen to the kidneys via sperm ducts. They are then carried through ureters and released out of the cloaca. On female frogs eggs are in the ovaries where they travel along oviducts to the uteri and are released through the cloaca. The male fertilizes the eggs externally and the mating behavior of the frogs is amplexus where the male climbs onto the female's back where the female frog then releases her eggs.
