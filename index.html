Intro

My name is Joseph B. Vadella and I am from Carbondale, Pennsylvania. I studied Economics and Political Science at the Pennsylvania State University where I graduated Cum Laude with a Bachelor of Science.
I am currently enrolled at the University of Hamburg in Germany and am pursuing a Masters in Economics.
Before deciding to continue higher education, I worked at Region 1 Planning Council in Rockford Illinois for two years as their primary data analyst.

Work

Below is a list of notable works I've been a part of.

Winnebago County Community Mental Health Board

The people of Winnebago County voted by referendum in 2019 to add a tax of 0.5% to every sale to pay for a government program to increase mental health care access in the county. I was the main data analyst responsible for this project. All administration of the what would become the Winnebago County Community Mental Health Board was contracted to Region 1 Planning Council's Community Impact Team. As a part of that team, I was the one who prepared the summary statistics for the funded programs, analyzed their reports and reported data, produced data visualizations of this data, and then prepared the final reports on their progress for the County Board and the general public.
A selection of reports that I did the data analysis and visualization work for are available below.

Community Lead Deliberation Event

This was an event me and 4 other students lead as part of our Rhetoric and Civic Life course while at Penn State. We reached out to community leaders in healthcare and criminal justice as well as the general public to congregate and deliberate solutions to the ongoing Opiod Epidemic. I was in charge in making the informational packet summarizing the talking points prepared by my groupmates. Link to the PDF here


The Great Debate | Penn State Econ Association

This is an annual event in which 6 students are split into right-wing and left-wing teams and make presentations on a chosen topic. My team was designated the "Socialism" side in the "Capitalism v. Socialism" debate in 2019. There was sadly no recording but I and my team won by a landslide in the post-debate audience poll. There was an amazing turnout of around 500 Penn State economics students (and my parents).
